Understanding Protein Test Parameters in Detail

Protein levels in the blood play a crucial role in assessing overall health, liver function, immune system activity, and nutritional status. Below is a deeper look at the different protein test parameters and what they indicate:

Total Protein (g/dL)

Normal Range: 6.4 – 8.3 g/dL

Total protein is the sum of albumin and globulin levels. It reflects liver function, kidney function, and nutritional health.

Low Total Protein: May indicate malnutrition, kidney disease, liver dysfunction, or gastrointestinal disorders.

High Total Protein: Could suggest chronic infections, inflammatory diseases, or multiple myeloma.

Albumin (g/dL)

Normal Range: 3.4 – 5.4 g/dL

Albumin is produced by the liver and plays a role in maintaining fluid balance, transporting nutrients, and repairing tissues.

Low Albumin: Suggests liver disease, kidney disease, chronic inflammation, or malnutrition.

High Albumin: Usually rare but can be seen in dehydration.

Importance of Protein Tests

These protein-related blood tests provide valuable insights into liver health, immune function, nutritional deficiencies, and inflammatory conditions. If any of these markers are abnormal, further medical evaluation may be necessary.

When should you get tested?

  • If you experience fatigue, swelling, frequent infections, or weight loss.
  • If you have a history of liver disease, kidney disease, or immune disorders.
  • As part of routine health check-ups or pre-operative assessments.
